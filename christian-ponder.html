Christian Ponder is a well-known former American football quarterback. Christian Ponder is best known for playing for Minnesota Vikings.

Early Life and Childhood

Christian Ponder was born Christian Andrew Ponder on 25th February 1988. His birth took place in Dallas, Texas, United States. By this, he is currently 34 years old and American by nationality. In addition, he follows Christianity and his zodiac sign is Aquarius.

Moving on towards his family members, he was born to his father David Ponder, and his mother Christine Ponder. His father was also a football player who played for Florida State in his college days. According to Christian, his dad influenced him to make a career in football. In terms of his educational status, he did his schooling at Colleyville Heritage High School. Then, he joined Florida State University and is one of their most valuable offensive players. He graduated from the university with a degree in finance.

Professional Life and Career

Christian started his football career in college. In 2007, he played for Drew Weatherford as the back-up behind. Likewise, in the 2009 season, he had a shoulder injury after playing eight games, and therefore, missed the rest of the season. In 2010, he again injured his elbow more than three times. However, his 2010 season was the most successful college career. In the year, he won the James Tatum Award and National Football Foundation Scholar-Athletic Award.

Minnesota Vikings selected him in the 2011 NFL draft. He had a rookie contract of $10 million guaranteed with the team. He made his NFL debut playing against Chicago Bears in 2011. However, the team lost the match. His first win in NFL was against Carolina Panthers in October 2011. The first game of the 2012 season was against Jacksonville Jaguars. Though he had his best game, the team lost the match. In the finale, Vikings hosted the division Champion Packers.

Ponder played the 2013 season as starting quarterback. The team lost the match with Chicago Bears but the players of Chicago were impressed by Ponder’s performance. He missed the week four game against Pittsburgh due to a rib injury. Therefore, he had a disappointing 2013 season. This is why his future with Viking fell in doubt. Though the team rendered him to third-string quarterback, he still wished to stay with the Vikings. However, Vikings chose not to extend his contract, he left Vikings in 2015.

Ponder signed a one-year contract with Oakland Raiders in March 2013. After preseason, Oakland released him. Then, he started gaining interest from Philadelphia Eagles, but he didn’t sign. In November 2015, he signed with Denver Broncos as the third-string quarterback. Likewise, in 2016, he signed with the San Francisco 49ers. Moreover, he was active as the backup quarterback for the final 3 games of the season.
