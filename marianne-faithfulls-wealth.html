Marianne Evelyn Gabriel Faithfull – A Brief Biography

Marianne Evelyn Faithfull, born on 29 December 1946, is a renowned English singer, songwriter, and actress with a career spanning over five decades. Her early career in pop and rock music during the 1960s was overshadowed by her battle with drug abuse in the 1970s. During the first two-thirds of that decade, she only produced two relatively unnoticed studio albums.

After a considerable hiatus, she made a noteworthy return in late 1979 with the highly acclaimed album, “Broken English”. Faithfull’s subsequent solo work, often praised by critics, has at times been overshadowed by her personal history.

Notably, from 1966 to 1970, she was involved in a highly publicized romantic relationship with Mick Jagger, the lead singer of the Rolling Stones. She co-wrote “Sister Morphine”, featured on the Rolling Stones’ Sticky Fingers album.
