The current UFC Welterweight Champion is British professional mixed martial artist Leon Edwards, who was born in Jamaica. He fights in the Welterweight class of the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC). Although he made his amateur debut at Bushido Challenge 2 – A New Dawn in 2010, the same year that he started his professional fighting career. He goes by the name Rocky. He is ranked #2 in the UFC welterweight standings as of April 12, 2022.

Timeline of Leon Edwards’s Career

  • Edwards made his amateur debut at Bushido Challenge 2 – A New Dawn in 2010 before turning professional.
  • In round two, he defeated Carl Booth by armbar submission.
  • He subsequently signed a contract with Fight UK MMA and made his professional debut there against Damian Zlotnicki, defeating him by technical knockout in the opening frame.
  • He then competed in two more fights for the company, defeating Delroy McDowell by DQ due to an illegal knee and defeating Pawel Zwiefka.
  • He finished his time at Fight UK MMA with a 2-1 record.
  • He then faced Craig White, defeating him by decision.
  • He then went to fight for “BAMMA,” winning all five of his fights. He defeated Wayne Murrie to win the welterweight championship, and in his final match with BAMMA before joining the UFC, he defeated Shaun Taylor to make one defense of the title.
  • On November 8, 2014, at UFC Fight Night: Shogun vs. Saint Preux, he competed in his first UFC promotional fight against Claudio Silva, which he lost.
