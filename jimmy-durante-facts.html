The Life and Legacy of Jimmy Durante: The Schnozzola of Showbiz

Jimmy Durante was one of the most recognizable entertainers of the 20th century. Known for his gravelly voice, wry humor, and most notably his distinctive nose, Durante was a singer, comedian, and actor who left a lasting impact on the entertainment industry. In this article, we’ll dive into the life, career, and legacy of Jimmy Durante and explore some of the fascinating facts about this beloved performer.

A Brief Bio

James Francis Durante was born on February 10, 1893, in New York City. Durante’s parents were Italian immigrants, and he grew up in poverty on the Lower East Side of Manhattan. Despite his humble beginnings, Durante showed an early interest in performance and began playing piano in saloons and vaudeville theaters at a young age.

Durante rose to fame during the 1920s and 1930s as a singer and comedian. He made his first recordings in 1923 and went on to become a major star of radio, television, and film. Over the course of his career, Durante earned numerous accolades, including an Emmy Award for his television work and induction into both the Songwriters Hall of Fame and the Big Band and Jazz Hall of Fame.

Age, Relationships, and Children

Durante was married to his wife, Jeanne Olsen, for over 60 years, until his death in 1980. The couple did not have any children of their own, but they were both known for their philanthropic efforts on behalf of children’s charities. Durante was also close friends with many other well-known performers of his era, including Frank Sinatra, Bing Crosby, and Ed Sullivan.

Height

One of the most striking features of Jimmy Durante’s appearance was his nose, which was large and bulbous. Although Durante was often self-deprecating about his appearance, he used his distinctive feature to comedic effect in his performances, earning him the moniker “Schnozzola.” Durante was not particularly tall, standing at just 5’7”.

Career

Durante’s career spanned over 60 years, and he worked in a variety of mediums during that time. In addition to his successful music career, he also appeared in numerous films and television shows, including the classic Christmas movie “It’s a Wonderful Life.” Durante was also a fixture in Las Vegas, where he performed regularly at The Sands Hotel and Casino.

Top Songs

Durante is best remembered for his classic songs, some of which have become iconic in American music history. Some of his most famous tunes include “Inka Dinka Doo,” “As Time Goes By,” and “Make Someone Happy.” His signature ragtime piano style and gravelly vocals made him stand out from other performers of his era, and his music continues to be appreciated by fans today.

Net Worth and Legacy

Jimmy Durante passed away on January 29, 1980, at the age of 86. At the time of his death, his net worth was estimated to be around $5 million. Although Durante was known for his comedic prowess, he was also a devoted humanitarian who supported numerous charities throughout his life. Today, his legacy lives on through his music, films, and the many causes he supported.

In Conclusion

In a career that spanned over six decades, Jimmy Durante made an indelible impact on the entertainment industry. With his unique voice, distinctive appearance, and quick wit, he was one of the biggest stars of his era. Although he is no longer with us, his music and legacy continue to entertain and inspire fans around the world.
