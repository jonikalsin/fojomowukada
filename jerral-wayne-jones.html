Professional Life and Career

Jones’s parents moved to Springfield, Missouri, USA, where Pat was president and chairman of Modern Security Life Insurance Co.

Similarly, after graduating from the University of Arkansas, he was listed as an executive vice president. And, Jones was co-captain of Arkansas’ 1964 national championship football team.

Moreover, Jones is one of a very small number of NFL owners who had a significant level of success as a football player.

As per an interview with Jones on HBO, after graduating from college in 1965, he borrowed a million dollars from Jimmy Hoffa’s Teamsters Union to open up a string of Shakey’s Pizza Parlor restaurants in Missouri.

And, when that venture failed, he was given a job at his father’s insurance company, Modern Security Life of Springfield.

Further, he received his master’s degree in business in the year 1970. After many other unsuccessful business ventures, he began an oil and gas exploration business in Arkansas, Jones Oil, and Land Lease, which became successful.

Moreover, his privately held company currently does natural resource prospecting.

In the year 2008, this personality formed a partnership with Yankee Global Enterprises to create Legends Hospitality, a food, beverage, merchandise, retail, and stadium operations corporation serving entertainment venues.

Dallas Cowboys

Jones purchased the Cowboys from H.R. “Bum” Bright for $140 million on February 25, 1989. After the purchase, he soon fired longtime coach Tom Landry.

After a few months, he fired longtime general manager Tex Schramm and assumed complete control over football matters.

Further, at the time of the sale, the financially troubled Bright claimed to be losing $1 million per month on the franchise. Moreover, during Jones’ tenure, the Cowboys have appreciated to an estimated $4.2 billion, turning their owner into a billionaire in the process

Moreover, after a slow start under Jones and Johnson, Jones quickly built a team often considered the best NFL franchise of the 1990s. The 2020 NFL season was Jones’ 32nd as Cowboys owner, over the combined tenures of his predecessors.
